Hero #1 · The Bouncer

Locky

Imagine: a fitting room with one door. You step in, Locky locks it and stands guard. Everyone else waits outside — nobody ends up wearing the same pants at the same time.
Superpower: pessimistic locking — SELECT the seat's row FOR UPDATE and hold it until you're done. Totally safe. But if a million people want the same door, the line gets long.
“One at a time — I hold the door.”
🏷️
Hero #2 · The Sticker Checker

Versa

Imagine: a garage sale. Anyone can grab the robot toy — no lines. But at checkout, the clerk compares your sticker number to the master list. Someone bought it while you wandered? Your number is old. Back you go.
Superpower: optimistic locking — every row carries a version number. Save only succeeds if the version hasn't changed; otherwise, retry. No waiting lines. Perfect when clashes are rare.
“Check the number — if it changed, try again.”
⏳
Hero #3 · The Seat Saver

Holdy

Imagine: your friend drops a jacket on a seat — saved! But not forever. If you're not back when the lights go down, the jacket comes off and the seat is anyone's.
Superpower: the reservation pattern — click a seat and it's HELD for ten minutes while you calmly pay. Pay in time, it's yours; miss it, the hold expires and the seat goes back on sale. The real ticket-site answer.
“Ten minutes. Pay up, or the seat goes back.”
🧾
Hero #4 · The Receipt Keeper

Echo

Imagine: you order pizza #42, the line glitches, and you nervously call again. Does the shop make two pizzas? No — they check the order number and say “already in the oven.”
Superpower: idempotency keys — every payment carries a unique key. If the same key arrives twice (a retry, a double-tap), the server returns the SAME receipt instead of charging again. Retries become harmless.
“Ask me twice — I answer once.”
📓
Hero #5 · The Diary Keeper

Loggy

Imagine: a chef clips the order ticket to the rail BEFORE cooking. Power blinks, kitchen forgets everything — the tickets are still on the rail. Nothing is lost; just cook down the list.
Superpower: the write-ahead log — before changing anything, append the plan to a diary on disk: “sell seat 12 to Alice.” Crash mid-way? Replay the diary on restart. Hidden inside almost every database on Earth.
“Write it down first — then do it.”
🪞
Hero #6 · The Copycat

Mirror

Imagine: the teacher writes on the front board; helpers copy it onto boards all around the room so five hundred kids can read without crowding one wall. The copies run a moment behind.
Superpower: leader-follower replication — all writes go to one leader; copies flow to many followers, and millions read the seat map from the copies. Careful: a copy can be a second stale — read your OWN purchase from the leader.
“One writes, many read — copies run a step behind.”

what beat the race
1. Never trust check-then-act. The world changes in the gap — lock or version the row.
2. Humans are slow — hold, don't lock. Timed reservations let people pay calmly.
3. Retries WILL happen. Idempotency keys make asking twice safe.
4. Log first, undo across systems. Diaries and sagas make crashes boring.
