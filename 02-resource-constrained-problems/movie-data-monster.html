Hero #1 · The Streamer

Chunky

Imagine: trying to swallow a whole gallon milkshake in one gulp. You'd explode! But through a straw, sip by sip, you can finish ANY size milkshake.
Superpower: reads the file through a straw — one small chunk at a time. Process a chunk, throw it away, sip the next. Memory stays tiny no matter how big the file is.
“Never swallow the whole file — sip it, gulp by gulp.”
🗂️
Hero #2 · The Librarian

Sorty

Imagine: a mountain of returned library books. Sorty doesn't sort the mountain — she sorts one small cart at a time, labels it, and parks it in the hallway.
Superpower: sorts RAM-sized piles and saves each sorted pile to disk. This is external merge sort, step one — the trick for sorting data way bigger than memory.
“Sort small piles first — staple the master list later.”
🤹
Hero #3 · The Juggler

Heapy

Imagine: a juggler who can only hold 10 balls. A new ball flies in? He instantly tosses the smallest one. Whatever happens, he's always holding the 10 biggest.
Superpower: the min-heap. Finds the top-K of ANYTHING — songs, hashtags — while remembering only K things. Also staples Sorty's piles together, always picking the smallest front card first.
“Always toss out the smallest one first.”
📮
Hero #4 · The Mail Sorter

Patch

Imagine: a mailroom with 100 cubbyholes. Every letter for Maple Street goes in cubby 7 — every single time. Want Maple Street's mail? Open ONE cubby, not the whole room.
Superpower: hash partitioning. Splits a giant file into cubbies by key, so matching items always land together. Then you process one small cubby at a time — dedup, group, join, easy.
“Same address, same cubby — every single time.”
👻
Hero #5 · The Watchdog

Ghost

Imagine: a doorman who's seen a billion faces but weighs less than a feather. Ask “has this one been here before?” — he answers instantly. Once in a blue moon he says “yes” by mistake, but he NEVER misses a repeat.
Superpower: the Bloom filter. Checks “have I seen this?” across billions of items using just megabytes — by trading a tiny bit of certainty for a mountain of memory.
“I almost never forget — and I barely take up any room.”
🚩
Hero #6 · The Scribe

Checkpoint

Imagine: a video game with no save points — the boss beats you at level 9, and you restart from level 1. Checkpoint plants a flag at every level, so you respawn right where you fell.
Superpower: writes down progress as the job runs — “finished chunk 900 of 1000.” Crash, power cut, coffee spill? The job resumes from the last flag, not from zero.
“If we get knocked down, we don't start from zero.”

what beat the monster
1. Stream, don't swallow. Sip the file in chunks — memory stays flat.
2. Spill to disk. Sorted piles and cubbies wait patiently on disk.
3. Trade a little exactness for a LOT of memory. That's Ghost.
4. Save your progress. Big jobs crash — checkpoints make crashes boring.
